
SOCIAL MEDIA DO’S AND DON’TS FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS

The 5 Do’s

Updating profile with correct time for making it look more professional . Online presence matters same like your real-life reputation, so keep it professional and clean for future opportunities.
Check your privacy settings regularly. Not everything needs to be public—protect your personal space.
Verify information before sharing. Being responsible online means not spreading misinformation.
Use social media to network with purpose. Connect with people who inspire you and can help you grow professionally.
Pause before you respond. If something upsets you, take a moment instead of reacting instantly—sometimes it’s better not to reply at all.
Do share your achievements thoughtfully. Whether it’s a project, internship, or certification, let your work speak about your strength and professionalism.
Do follow pages and communities that add value and interest . Surround your feed with content that helps you learn and stay motivated.

The 5 Don’ts

Don’t post anything you wouldn’t be comfortable showing your family. Once it’s online, it stays there.
Don’t speak negatively about your college, professors, or classmates. It can come back to affect your reputation.
Don’t share your live location publicly. Prioritize your safety at all times.
Don’t get pulled into online arguments. It rarely leads to anything positive and can harm your image.
Don’t trust “disappearing” messages. Screenshots exist, and nothing online is ever truly temporary.
Don’t compare your journey with others online. Social media often shows highlights, not the full reality vwhich creates confusion and doubts
Don’t spend excessive time scrolling and wasting time online. It can affect your productivity and focus on important goals.
