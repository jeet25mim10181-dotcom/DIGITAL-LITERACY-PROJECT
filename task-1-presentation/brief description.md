My Task 1 deliverable was a Digital Literacy Essentials infographic. My overall approach was to take traditional, tried-and-true advice — like knowing good strangers from bad or being organized — and translate it into the digital age.

The infographic consists of three simple parts. The first section of the paper puts a definition to what digital literacy means and how successful use is actually enacted in practice. In the second part, we explain some of the tools students often depend on — Evernote to help organize notes; Google Calendar for scheduling and time management; Zoom for communication and collaboration.

The last part emphasizes the key online safety rules to follow. We take precautions to be safe in the real world, but being careful online is just as important. This means creating strong passwords, not opening strange links and doing everything to keep your information safe.
