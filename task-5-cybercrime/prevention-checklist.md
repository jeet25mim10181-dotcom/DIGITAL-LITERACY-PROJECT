
Online Safety Checklist 

Use unique, strong passwords for each account and enable 2-factor-authentication (2FA) whenever possible.Immediately contact your bank to block the transaction 
if you receive a password that does not originate from you, an OTP, or a message related to UPI PIN debit. Never share your OTP, passwords, or UPI PIN
with anyone—even people claiming to be from your bank.
Always double-check the authenticity of any payment request before approving it. Remember, entering your UPI PIN signifies that you're authorizing a payment,
not receiving funds.

Avoid scanning unknown QR codes or clicking on fraudulent payment links.
Only download apps from official stores (Google Play Store/Apple App Store), and review app feedback before downloading.Do not do banking or sensitive transactions 
on public Wi-Fi; use your mobile data or a secure network.Keep your phone, apps, and antivirus software up-to-date to guard against security holes.Regularly 
check your bank statements and transaction history, as this may help you notice any unauthorized activity sooner.Warning about “too good to be true” offers, 
job scams or phony buyer/seller messages on OLX/Instagram platforms.Sign out of shared systems and don’t save passwords on public or borrowed machines.
If you are the victim of cyber fraud or suspect a scam:

Immediate Reporting On National Cyber Crime Portal — https://cybercrime.gov.in

Contact the Cyber Crime Helpline : 1930

Project Report (100–150 words)

The most surprising thing to me, when researching cybercrime though, was how even simple tricks — for instance sending a QR code or asking for a
UPI PIN — can lead to people incurring an instant loss. I wasn’t aware that so many people still confuse “receiving” with “sending” money in UPI transactions,
an act that scammers can easily exploit. It was also surprising how quickly those who will commit fraud generations panic or urgency to victims forces them to 
make decisions without thinking.From this research, personally one habit I will change is to be more considerate before accepting any online payment request.
I will verify every transaction, be avoid through QR codes I don’t know where they come from and never share confidential information like OTPs or PINs. 
Having this awareness hopefully will keep me more vigilant, so I won't succumb to some digital bait and switch.
